Furniture care guide

Caring for solid-wood furniture in heated or air-conditioned homes.

Expertise draft · Furniwell Indonesia

Solid wood responds to changes in indoor humidity. Stable room conditions, prompt spill cleanup, and distance from direct heat or strong air-conditioning vents can reduce unnecessary movement, surface damage, and joint stress.

Control the environment

Avoid placing solid-wood furniture directly beside radiators, heating outlets, fireplaces, or continuous cold-air vents. Sudden local changes are more damaging than gradual seasonal movement.

Clean without saturating the surface

Use a soft dry or slightly damp cloth. Wipe spills immediately and avoid leaving standing water on joints, edges, and unfinished surfaces.

Follow the approved finish system

Oiled, lacquered, painted, and waxed surfaces require different maintenance. Buyers should request the exact care instruction associated with the approved production finish.

The 6–8% moisture-content claim from the execution document is intentionally excluded until Furniwell provides verifiable production evidence.

Ask for product-specific instructions

For hospitality or commercial projects, include cleaning agents, expected room conditions, and maintenance frequency in the original product specification.
