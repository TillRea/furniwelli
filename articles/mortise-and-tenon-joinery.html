Why Mortise-and-Tenon Joinery Survives Ocean Freight

Expertise draft · Furniwell Indonesia

A mortise-and-tenon joint locks two timbers together with a carved tongue (tenon) seated inside a matching socket (mortise), far stronger than screws or staples. For furniture shipped across oceans in changing temperature and humidity, this traditional joint flexes without failing, which is why Furniwell uses it on tables, chairs, and case goods built for export. Combined with 6-8% kiln-dried timber, it is the backbone of furniture that arrives intact and stays solid.
